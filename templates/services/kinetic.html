Cupping flushes out "gunk" from your muscles. To help your body get rid of it, you’ll need to drink plenty of water for the next 24 hours. Think of it as a internal rinse.

The area might feel slightly warm or tender, almost like a mild sunburn, for a few hours. This is normal—it means fresh blood is rushing in to repair the tissue.

The best part: you will feel much "lighter." Many clients say they feel like they’ve lost five pounds of tension or that their joints finally have "room" to move.

NATHALIE’S TIP: Keep the treated areas covered and warm. Avoid cold showers, swimming, or very cold air for 12 hours. Your muscles are "open" and prefer to stay cozy while they recover.
